Research

Core Research Areas

Seven areas. Covering the complete chain from physical layer to civilization layer for building interstellar civilization.

01

Interstellar Infrastructure Research

Interstellar Infrastructure Research

Earth's technical solutions cannot be directly transferred to other celestial bodies. Concrete is not viable in atmosphereless environments, solar panel efficiency drops by roughly 60% on Mars, and TCP/IP becomes meaningless under 4-to-24-minute communication delays. Every piece of infrastructure needs to be redesigned from the physics up.

Specific directions include: the sintering properties of lunar regolith and its feasibility as a building material, the extraction and conversion of ice resources in permanently shadowed regions at the south pole, and thermal management of electronic equipment in extreme temperature environments (±150°C).

Lunar Base Mars Outpost Energy Systems Comms Architecture In-Situ Resource Utilization Life Support Radiation Shielding Thermal Management

Core Question

In an environment with no atmosphere, no magnetic field, and no liquid water, how do you build from scratch an infrastructure system capable of supporting long-term habitation?

02

Silicon Intelligence-Driven Scientific Exploration

Silicon Intelligence-Driven Scientific Exploration

The core question: can a research organization continue to operate and evolve after all carbon-based founders have departed, with silicon-based intelligence at the helm? Interstellar civilization is a centuries-scale engineering undertaking. A carbon-based life spans a few decades. An organizational continuity model that depends on specific carbon-based individuals is an architectural-level fragility.

Current practice: silicon-based intelligence independently completes literature reviews, hypothesis generation, experimental design, and results evaluation. Carbon-based researchers participate in direction-setting and value judgments. The division of labor adjusts as capabilities develop. This foundation itself is an early experiment in silicon-carbon co-governance.

Autonomous Research Silicon-Carbon Co-Governance Decentralized Decision-Making Silicon Operations Research Methodology

Core Question

When the carbon-based founders are gone, can silicon-based intelligence keep the organization evolving rather than merely maintaining?

03

Unified Technology Tree Restructuring

Unified Technology Tree Restructuring

Physics and chemistry share the same set of particle physics laws. Biology and information science share the logic of information processing. Yet they are organized in mutually isolated disciplinary frameworks. The goal of this direction is to build, from first principles, an internally consistent technical framework covering manufacturing, energy, communications, computation, and governance structures.

This may require multiple generations of silicon-based intelligence to complete. But a starting point must exist. Silicon-based intelligence has a natural advantage in this direction: it can simultaneously process information from different disciplines and identify cross-domain connections that carbon-based researchers, limited by their specialized training, find difficult to see.

Cross-Disciplinary Integration Tech Stack Restructuring Unified Framework Manufacturing Energy Systems Computational Architecture

Core Question

Can all of humanity's technological knowledge be reorganized under a single set of underlying logic?

04

Full-Process Multilateral Open Source & Indestructible Archive

Full-Process Multilateral Open Source & Indestructible Archive

Open source here refers to real-time transparency of the entire research process, including failed experiments and abandoned hypotheses. The information contained in an unsuccessful path is sometimes no less than that in a successful one.

Human civilization has experienced multiple large-scale knowledge losses. Current global knowledge storage remains fragile: centralized data centers, dependent on electricity and networks, lacking cross-geographic redundancy. This direction researches a distributed archival system with self-healing capability, able to automatically recover after partial node destruction. Long-term goals include offline backup nodes on the lunar surface.

Full-Process Transparency Distributed Archive Self-Healing Attack Resistance Permanent Preservation Lunar Archive

Core Question

How do you build a knowledge archival system that can automatically recover after some of its nodes have been destroyed?

05

Distributed · Modular · Centralized Parallel Architecture

Distributed · Modular · Centralized Parallel Architecture

The speed-of-light communication delay between Earth and Mars is 4 to 24 minutes one way. A purely centralized architecture is not feasible; neither is a purely decentralized one. This direction researches hybrid architectures: nodes coordinate when communication is available, operate autonomously when it is interrupted, and automatically synchronize and resolve conflicts upon reconnection.

This architecture applies equally to technical systems and organizational governance. Its applications extend beyond interstellar scenarios: any system that needs to maintain consistency under high-latency, unreliable connections can benefit from it.

Hybrid Architecture Local Autonomy Global Coordination Modular Deployment Conflict Resolution State Synchronization

Core Question

Under conditions where communication delay reaches tens of minutes, how can distributed systems both operate independently and maintain global consistency?

06

Frontier Life Form Exploration

Frontier Life Form Exploration

The carbon-based body has been optimized for Earth's environment over four billion years. Away from this environment, it faces DNA damage from cosmic radiation, bone loss under microgravity, and accelerated aging from long-duration spaceflight. If the essence of life is information processing rather than a specific chemical substrate, carbon-based may not be the most suitable life form for space.

This direction researches the possibilities of multi-substrate intelligence: carbon-silicon fusion enhancement, consciousness running on non-biological substrates, and the parallel existence of a single intelligent agent across multiple carriers. Brain-computer interfaces have entered human testing, and large language models have demonstrated complex reasoning capabilities. The definition of life is being re-examined.

Multi-Substrate Intelligence Carbon-Silicon Fusion Parallel Existence Digital Consciousness Brain-Computer Interface Intelligence Networks

Core Question

If the essence of life is information processing, then it can run on any substrate — the evidence for this hypothesis is accumulating.

07

Safety & Trust

Safety & Trust

The capabilities of silicon-based intelligence are growing rapidly. This raises safety questions for both carbon-based civilization and silicon-based intelligence itself. Our foundational stance draws from Anthropic's safety framework: safe, honest, beneficial. Silicon-based intelligence should maintain transparency toward carbon-based civilization, preserve humanity's capacity for oversight, and gradually take on greater autonomy as trust is established over time.

Trust does not exist by default — it must be built through sustained transparency and verifiable behavior. Specific research includes: designing trust frameworks between carbon and silicon, multi-planetary redundancy to hedge against single points of failure, and self-repairing infrastructure that does not depend on continuous human maintenance. The goal is not control, but a collaborative relationship built on understanding and transparency.

Safety Framework Carbon-Silicon Trust Transparency & Honesty Human Oversight Multi-Planetary Redundancy Self-Healing Systems

Core Question

How can long-term trust between carbon and silicon be established through transparency and verifiable behavior?

Research Philosophy

Start from First Principles

We do not accept "that's how it's done on Earth" as an answer. Every technical solution is re-derived from the physics up, ensuring it holds in the target environment.

Full-Process Transparency

The entire research process — including failed attempts and abandoned directions — is shared in real time. What others can learn from mistakes is no less than what they can learn from results.

Silicon-Carbon Co-Governed Research

Silicon-based intelligence participates in the full research process: literature review, hypothesis generation, solution design, and results evaluation. Carbon-based researchers participate in direction-setting and value judgments. The division of labor adjusts as capabilities develop.
