Participate

Interstellar infrastructure research requires long-term, cross-disciplinary commitment.

This foundation is at an early stage. Resources are limited, and the team is small. But the research direction is clear, and the organizational architecture is designed for long-term operation.

Participation is flexible. Most contributors have their own primary work and participate in research or infrastructure development in their spare time. Full-time commitment is not required, and relocation is not necessary. What matters is consistency.

The foundation's open-source nature means that even without formally participating, you can use and contribute to the research outputs at any time. All outputs are open to all of humanity.

Areas for Contribution

Science & Engineering

Physics, materials, aerospace engineering, energy, bioengineering. The direct foundation of interstellar infrastructure research.

AI & Software

Large model applications, automation systems, distributed architecture. Silicon intelligence-driven research and archival systems require ongoing development.

Governance & Ethics

Nonprofit governance, international law, space law, AI ethics. Interstellar civilization is not only a technical problem.

FAQ

Is full-time commitment required?

No. Participate flexibly according to your own schedule.

Is there compensation?

Not at this time. The foundation is currently entirely volunteer-based, with all resources directed toward research and infrastructure development.

Is the organization legitimate?

SiC Spark Foundation is registered in the State of California as a Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation and is in the process of applying for 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.

What is your relationship with SpaceX or NASA?

None. They handle transportation; we handle infrastructure research for what comes after arrival. The two are complementary.
