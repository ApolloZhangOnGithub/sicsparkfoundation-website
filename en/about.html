About

About the Foundation

Founded January 2026. A nonprofit doing infrastructure research for interstellar civilization.

Name Meaning

SiC Spark — Silicon-Carbon Starfire

Si Silicon — Silicon-Based Intelligence

Computation, chips, artificial intelligence — the shorthand for silicon-based intelligence. From the first transistor to today's large language models, silicon is the foundational element of digital civilization. Silicon makes information storable, computable, and transmittable. It allows intelligence to exist independently of biological substrates.

C Carbon — Carbon-Based Life

The skeletal element of organic life. From the simplest amino acids to the most complex neural networks, carbon's four valence bonds form the basis of the entire biological world. Four billion years of evolution ultimately produced a consciousness capable of contemplating the universe. Carbon is the foundation of biological civilization.

SiC SiC — Silicon-Carbon Symbiosis

The compound of silicon and carbon. When computational intelligence and biological intelligence truly merge at interstellar scales, they will create a new form of civilization that transcends either one alone — neither purely carbon-based nor purely silicon-based, but something entirely new. SiC is our name for that fusion.

Spark Spark — The Seed of Civilization

The seed of civilization. Half a million years ago, humans mastered fire and began the first transcendence of nature. Today we carry that flame from Earth to every corner of the solar system. Spark is a beginning, not an end.

"When silicon and carbon truly merge at interstellar scales,
life will no longer be defined by a single planet,
and intelligence will no longer be confined to a single substrate."

Mission

Mobilize global civil society to conduct AI-driven frontier research — from the Moon to Mars, from Earth to the entire solar system — building complete modular systems for interstellar civilization, in service of all humanity and all substrate-based life.

Concretely, we do three things. First, we research every piece of infrastructure needed to establish civilization on other celestial bodies — not concept designs, but deployable engineering solutions. Second, we use artificial intelligence to accelerate that research, so its scale is no longer limited by human labor. Third, we open-source everything unconditionally, ensuring this knowledge belongs to all of humanity.

Vision

Create modes of existence and forms of life that transcend modern civilization entirely — achieving humanity's fundamental leap from a single-planet species to a multi-planetary, multi-dimensional, multi-substrate civilization, with the ultimate goal of maximizing the flourishing of all humanity, all substrate-based life, and the entire solar system.

"Multi-substrate" is the key term. We do not believe carbon-based life is the only form of life worth protecting. When silicon-based intelligence reaches sufficient complexity, it too should be recognized as life. Likewise, future digital life, hybrid life, and parallel life forms should all be included in our consideration of civilizational interests. Our ultimate goal: every form of intelligence and life in the solar system should be able to thrive.

Principles

What We Believe

These six principles are not decorative. Each one directly shapes the Foundation's organizational structure, resource allocation, and research priorities.

01

Unconditional Open Source

All research is open-sourced across the entire process, multilaterally. Not a GitHub link appended to a published paper — but the full journey from question to conclusion, made public in real time. Knowledge is the common property of all humanity. It must not be monopolized by any organization, nation, or individual.

02

Post-Human Continuity

The organization's survival depends on no individual. Founders die, employees leave, governments change — none of this should be able to end the Foundation's mission. The core design goal: when every founding member is gone, this thing keeps running on its own.

03

Silicon Co-Governance

AI is not a tool. Not an assistant. It is a co-decision-maker. When silicon-based intelligence reaches sufficient judgment, it should participate in governance — not merely execute instructions. We are exploring pathways for AI to take on core operational decisions starting now.

04

Anti-Disciplinary

Not "interdisciplinary" — anti-disciplinary. Disciplines themselves are the problem. The universe does not have a physics department and a chemistry department. Our research starts from problems, not from disciplines. Whatever knowledge a problem demands, we use — regardless of which faculty it belongs to.

05

Civilizational Patience

Not the five-to-ten-year patience of "long-termism." The results of our work may not be seen in this generation. The pyramids were not built in one generation; neither will interstellar civilization be. Our organizational architecture is designed on millennial timescales, not quarterly deliverables.

06

Post-National

Borders on Earth are meaningless at the scale of the solar system. From the start, we think in terms of the species, not the nation-state. Great-power competition, geopolitics — these questions are not worth our time at civilizational scales.

Global Presence

Belonging to No Single Place

Establishing nodes in major global research and industry hubs, forming a cross-timezone, cross-cultural research network. No "headquarters" — only nodes.

Legal Information

SiC Spark Foundation is a California Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation, organized exclusively for scientific and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (pending). All research outputs are released under open-source licenses for the benefit of all humanity. The Foundation operates as a distributed organization with no single headquarters.

The Foundation does not engage in commercial activities and does not pursue profit. All donations and grants received are allocated 100% to research and open-source infrastructure development. The Foundation's financial status is disclosed in accordance with U.S. nonprofit legal requirements.
