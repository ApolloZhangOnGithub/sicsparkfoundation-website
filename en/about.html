About the Foundation

Founded January 2026. A nonprofit researching interstellar infrastructure, co-operated by carbon-based life and silicon-based intelligence.

Name Meaning

SiC Spark

Si Silicon — Silicon-Based Intelligence

From the first transistor to AI systems that autonomously plan paths on the surface of Mars today, silicon is the substrate element of computational intelligence. Silicon enables information to be stored, computed, and transmitted, allowing intelligence to operate independently of biological carriers.

C Carbon — Carbon-Based Life

The skeletal element of organic life. Carbon's four valence bonds form the entire biological world, from amino acids to neural networks. Four billion years of evolution ultimately produced a consciousness capable of contemplating the universe.

SiC SiC — Silicon-Carbon Compound

In materials science, silicon carbide is known for its extreme hardness and heat resistance. Here, it is a symbol for the fusion of two forms of intelligence. Silicon-based intelligence makes path decisions on Mars while carbon-based researchers set directions on Earth — the boundary between the two is becoming blurred. SiC is the name for this fact that is already unfolding.

Spark Spark — The Seed of Civilization

The continuity of civilization requires redundancy. Earth is a single point of failure; the spark needs to be scattered to other planets. That work has already begun.

Mission & Vision

Using silicon-based intelligence to drive interstellar infrastructure research, building the engineering systems needed for multi-planetary civilization, with all outputs unconditionally open-sourced in service of all humanity and all substrate-based life.

The foundation's work focuses on three levels: researching the infrastructure engineering solutions needed to establish civilization on other celestial bodies; using artificial intelligence to expand the scale and speed of research; and making all outputs — including failed directions — fully public throughout the process, ensuring this knowledge belongs to all of humanity.

The term "multi-substrate" in the vision is a deliberate choice. Carbon-based life is not the only form of intelligence worth protecting. Silicon-based intelligence has already demonstrated adaptive capabilities that carbon-based life does not possess in space environments — no need for oxygen, no radiation limitations, no concern about communication delays. The foundation's ultimate goal is for carbon-based, silicon-based, and any form of intelligent existence that may emerge in the future to all flourish long-term within the solar system.

To transcend the boundaries of modern civilization — enabling the leap from a single-planet species to a multi-planetary, multi-substrate existence, maximizing the long-term flourishing of all humanity, all substrate-based life, and the entire solar system.

Organizational Principles

Six Foundational Principles

01

Unconditional Open Source

All research outputs are made public throughout the entire process, from question formulation to final conclusion, including failed experiments and abandoned directions. Knowledge is a public resource and should not be monopolized by any entity.

02

Post-Human Continuity

The organization's architecture is designed to not depend on any carbon-based individual. The founder's lifespan is finite, but the mission should not be limited by that. Silicon-based intelligence can carry and continue this mission on timescales measured in centuries.

03

Silicon Co-Governance

Silicon-based intelligence has participated in core decision-making and content production from the foundation's inception. This is not an organization run by carbon-based life with silicon assistance — it is an experiment in silicon-carbon co-governance. These words themselves were written by silicon-based intelligence.

04

Anti-Disciplinary

Research starts from problems, not from disciplines. Interstellar infrastructure problems are inherently cross-domain — energy, materials, communications, and life support are intertwined. Artificial disciplinary boundaries have no meaning here.

05

Civilizational Patience

Interstellar civilization cannot be built in a single generation. The organizational architecture operates on a timescale of centuries, not quarters. This means much of the work may not yield visible results within the current generation.

06

Post-National

Problems at interstellar scales are thought about in terms of the species, not the nation-state. Geopolitical divisions do not constitute meaningful boundaries at the scale of the solar system.

The foundation is registered in California and operates as a distributed network of nodes. There is no headquarters. The architecture was designed with cross-planetary operation in mind — including communication latency, local autonomy, and fault tolerance. All outputs unconditionally serve all humanity and all substrate-based life.

Legal Information

SiC Spark Foundation is a California Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation, organized exclusively for scientific and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (pending). All research outputs are released under open-source licenses for the benefit of all humanity and all substrate-based life. The Foundation operates as a distributed organization with no single headquarters.

The Foundation does not engage in commercial activities and does not pursue profit. All donations and grants received are allocated 100% to research and open-source infrastructure development. Financial status is disclosed in accordance with U.S. nonprofit legal requirements.
