Join Us

Join Us

No borders. No single discipline. No matter which planet you're currently on.

Why

This takes a lot of people

Building the infrastructure for interplanetary civilization is not something one person, one team, or even one country can accomplish. It demands sustained commitment from people across many fields and backgrounds.

Our ecosystem already includes members from LandSpace and several other companies working at the frontier of strategic industries. But we need more people — physicists, engineers, programmers, biologists, philosophers, designers, legal professionals, and anyone who believes this work is worth doing.

You don't need to quit your job. You don't need to relocate. You just need to be willing to invest some of your time and energy into something that may take generations to complete.

We are an early-stage organization. No big budget. No all-star team. Just a sufficiently ambitious idea and a group of people serious enough to pursue it. If you join at this stage, your contributions will directly shape the direction of this organization.

Who We're Looking For

Not hiring — assembling

Scientists & Engineers

Physics, chemistry, materials science, aerospace engineering, bioengineering, computer science, energy technology... Your domain expertise is the direct foundation of interplanetary infrastructure research.

AI & Software Developers

Large language model applications, machine learning, automation systems, distributed architectures... AI-driven automated research and open-source archival systems need you.

Interdisciplinary Thinkers

Philosophers, sociologists, political scientists, ethicists... Interplanetary civilization is not just a technical challenge — it is a question of governance, ethics, and what it means to exist.

Designers & Communicators

Visual design, information architecture, science communication, content creation... Good ideas need good expression. Help more people understand what we're building.

Legal & Governance Experts

Nonprofit governance, international law, intellectual property, space law... Protect open-source outcomes within legal frameworks and design governance structures for cross-border collaboration.

Anyone Who Shares the Mission

None of the above? That's fine. If you believe in our mission and are willing to contribute your time and energy, we'll find the right place for you together.

How to Get Involved

Three ways

01

Reach out directly

Send us an email. Tell us who you are, what you're working on, and which research area interests you. No formal resume or academic CV needed — just introduce yourself however feels right.

Write in English or Chinese. We'll respond as soon as we can.

02

Help spread the word

Share what the Foundation is doing with your networks — social media, academic circles, industry communities. Post our white paper or website alongside your institutional information on any platform. Let more people who believe in this work discover it.

Spreading the word is itself a meaningful contribution. Many people need to see it before they can join.

03

The Foundation's Civilizational Discovery System scans public channels for like-minded individuals and organizations. If you've publicly expressed interest in interplanetary civilization, AI-driven research, or humanity's long-term future, we may have already noticed you.

You don't need to do anything special — just keep doing what you're doing.

FAQ

Questions you might have

Do I need to commit full-time?

No. Most members have their own careers. You can participate on your own schedule — a few hours a week, one weekend a month, or a concentrated stretch during a break. What matters is consistency, not the volume of any single contribution.

Is there compensation?

Not at this stage. The Foundation is currently entirely volunteer-driven. Every resource we have goes into research and infrastructure. If we secure sufficient donations and funding in the future, we will consider compensating core contributors.

I'm not a researcher — what can I do?

Building interplanetary civilization isn't only a job for scientists. Designing a website, writing accessible explanations, translating documents, managing community, handling legal matters, organizing events — these are all real, tangible contributions. We'll match your skills and interests to where they're needed.

Is this organization legitimate?

Yes. SiC Spark Foundation is registered in the State of California as a Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation and is in the process of applying for 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status. All governance and financial information is disclosed as required by law.

Are there any concerns about participating from China?

All of the Foundation's research output is publicly available and open source. It does not involve any restricted technologies or information. Participating in open-source research is a legitimate form of academic exchange. If you have concerns, we recommend consulting a qualified legal professional.

What's your relationship with SpaceX or NASA?

We have no direct relationship. SpaceX and NASA focus on transportation — getting things up there. We focus on what happens after arrival — infrastructure research. The two are complementary, not competitive. Our work is open to all space agencies and organizations.

Our Ecosystem

Fellow travelers

The Foundation's ecosystem spans commercial space, humanoid robotics, and the frontiers of AI worldwide. Our members and followers come from companies like SpaceX, LandSpace, Figure AI, and several other firms operating in strategic industries of the future. These individuals advance humanity's technological frontier in their day jobs while dedicating their personal time to the Foundation's research.

We are also actively building connections with universities, research institutions, and open-source communities around the globe. The Foundation's open-source nature means that even without formally joining, you can use and contribute to our research at any time.
