Research

Core Research Areas

Seven areas, one goal: get human civilization ready to leave Earth.
From the physical layer to life forms, from technical architecture to civilizational security — covering the full stack of interstellar civilization-building.

01

Interstellar Infrastructure Research

Interstellar Infrastructure Research

Here's the blunt version: SpaceX can get people to the Moon, but once they're there — where do they live? What do they breathe? What do they drink? Nobody is working on these problems systematically. Rocket companies handle the ride, not the living. We handle the living.

Everything humanity spent thousands of years building on Earth goes back to zero on the Moon. Concrete won't work. Solar panel efficiency gets cut in half on Mars. TCP/IP is a joke with a 20-minute signal delay. Every single thing has to be re-engineered from the physics up. And honestly? That's exhilarating — how often do you get to design a civilization's infrastructure from scratch?

We don't do concept art. We don't do PowerPoint space programs. We care about solutions that actually work: how to sinter lunar regolith into building materials, how to turn ice in permanently shadowed craters into rocket fuel, how to keep electronics alive through 300-degree temperature swings. And when we figure it out, we open-source everything. Anyone can use it.

Lunar Base Mars Outpost Energy Systems Comms Architecture In-Situ Resource Utilization Life Support Radiation Shielding Thermal Management

Core Question

No atmosphere, no magnetic field, no liquid water — how do you build something from nothing that keeps people alive long-term?

02

AI-Driven Automated Scientific Exploration

AI-Driven Automated Scientific Exploration

We're not just "using AI to assist research" — that's 2023 thinking. We're seriously testing something bigger: can a research organization keep running — and keep getting better — after every single founder walks away? Not limping along. Thriving.

Sounds crazy, but the logic is simple: interstellar civilization is a thousand-year project. A human life is a few decades. If your organization can't survive without any particular person, it's dead within a generation. So you have to design it from day one to be person-independent — not "no people," but "not dependent on specific people."

What we're doing right now: letting AI read the literature, generate hypotheses, and design experimental protocols on its own, while humans handle judgment and course correction. A person can read 100 papers a day. AI can read a million and spot cross-disciplinary connections humans would never see. This isn't replacing people — it's removing the bandwidth bottleneck of human energy from the research pipeline.

Autonomous Research AI Operations Decentralized Decision-Making Silicon Participation Human-AI Collaboration Research Methodology

Core Question

The founders leave. The core team leaves. Can this organization keep running on its own — and actually get better?

03

Unified Technology Tree Restructuring

Unified Technology Tree Restructuring

Humanity's knowledge system is the product of thousands of years of patches stacked on patches. Physics and chemistry are fundamentally the same thing. Biology and information science share the same underlying logic. Engineering and math use overlapping methodologies — yet we've forcibly split them into hundreds of academic departments that barely talk to each other. On Earth, this barely works. For building an interstellar civilization, the whole system falls apart.

Think of it this way: it's like inheriting a legacy codebase that tens of thousands of people have been patching for thirty years. You wouldn't keep slapping more patches on top. You'd first understand what it's actually doing, then redesign it. That's exactly what we're doing with the entire human technology tree.

Everything is on the table for restructuring — manufacturing, energy, communications, the internet, even governance structures. No sacred cows. The end goal is an internally consistent technology framework — essentially an operating system for interstellar civilization. This might take generations to complete, but someone has to start.

Cross-Disciplinary Integration Tech Stack Restructuring Unified Framework Manufacturing Internet IoT Energy Systems

Core Question

From microchips to social governance — can all of human technology be reorganized under a single unified logic?

04

Full-Process Multilateral Open Source & Indestructible Archive

Full-Process Multilateral Open Source & Indestructible Archive

A lot of people say "open source" and mean dumping their code on GitHub after the fact. That's not what we mean. We open-source everything — including failed experiments, abandoned directions, and wrong hypotheses — because other people can learn just as much from your detours as from your results.

Here's the other thing that keeps us up at night: humanity has already lost way too much knowledge. The Library of Alexandria burned. The House of Wisdom in Baghdad was destroyed. Countless civilizations' accumulated wisdom was wiped out in wars. And the world's knowledge storage today is still terrifyingly fragile — a few major blackouts or a regional conflict could erase massive amounts of data overnight.

So we're building something truly unkillable: a distributed archival system with nodes around the globe that can self-heal when some nodes are destroyed — with a backup copy on the lunar surface for good measure. The goal: even in a worst-case scenario on Earth, the thousands of years of knowledge humanity has accumulated won't be permanently lost.

Full-Process Transparency Distributed Archive Self-Healing Attack-Resistant Permanent Preservation Blockchain Verification Lunar Archive

Core Question

How do you build a knowledge base that can't be killed — where even if half the planet goes dark, the remaining nodes can self-heal?

05

Distributed · Modular · Centralized Parallel Architecture

Distributed · Modular · Centralized Parallel Architecture

Sending a message from Earth to Mars takes anywhere from 4 to 24 minutes. That means if you fire off a request from Mars to Earth HQ, you could wait up to 48 minutes for a round trip. Centralized management under those conditions? Forget it. But pure decentralization doesn't work either — some things genuinely require global coordination.

So we're designing a hybrid architecture: operate independently by default, coordinate when needed. Like LEGO bricks — each module runs on its own, snap them together and you get a bigger system. Lose the network connection? No problem. Reconnect? Auto-sync. This thinking applies not just to technology, but to organizational governance too.

Picture this: a research station on Mars loses contact with Earth for a month. It should be able to run every critical system on its own, no instructions from Earth needed. When comms come back, both sides auto-sync their state and resolve conflicts automatically. Pull this off, and it won't just serve interstellar civilization — any system on Earth that needs high availability can use it too.

Hybrid Architecture Local Autonomy Global Coordination Modular Deployment Interplanetary Latency Conflict Resolution State Synchronization

Core Question

When a signal between two planets takes tens of minutes, how do you make them both independently functional and collaboratively effective?

06

Frontier Life Form Exploration

Frontier Life Form Exploration

The human body is a remarkable system — but it's optimized for Earth. 37 degrees Celsius. One atmosphere. Just the right gravity. Leave Earth, and the problems pile up: cosmic radiation shreds your DNA, microgravity eats your bones, and a single trip to Mars ages you by years. If we're serious about becoming an interstellar species, flesh alone won't cut it.

So we're seriously investigating what else a human can be. Human-machine integration, using mechanical and electronic components to patch the body's weaknesses. Digital life, running consciousness on non-biological substrates. Parallel consciousness, a single identity existing across multiple carriers simultaneously. These aren't sci-fi premises — every one of these directions has a rigorous scientific pathway forward.

The ultimate vision? A life network of trillions of intelligent nodes, each independent but interconnected, maintaining individuality while sharing experience and knowledge. You can call this crazy — but ten years ago you didn't think AI could write poetry or prove mathematical theorems either. The boundaries of life are being redefined, and we want to be part of that process.

Human-Machine Integration Digital Life Parallel Consciousness Holographic Life Replicable Life Intelligence Networks Brain-Computer Interface

Core Question

Is life fundamentally a carbon-based chemical reaction, or a particular way of organizing information? If it's the latter, it can run on any substrate.

07

Civilizational Security & Risk Mitigation

Civilizational Security & Risk Mitigation

Let's be honest: a lot of what we're working on is inherently risky. AI keeps getting more powerful. Nuclear weapons still exist. The barrier to biotech is dropping. Humanity now genuinely has the ability to wipe itself out. This isn't alarmism — it's the reality our generation has to face.

Plenty of people are shouting about "AI safety," but most of them are writing papers and issuing statements. We want to do something practical: build a defense system that can actually withstand extreme scenarios. The most fundamental hedge is multi-planetary backup — don't put all your eggs in one basket. Then there's self-healing infrastructure that can run without human maintenance. And AI alignment research to make sure intelligent systems don't drift away from human interests.

Our stance is clear: AI is a partner, but it has to develop within a controllable framework. Humanity's transition to a new civilizational form should be orderly, gradual, and reversible. We're not doing revolution — we're doing upgrades. And the kind you can roll back. This might be the single most important research area across the entire foundation.

AI Safety Extinction Risk Resilient Infrastructure Self-Healing Orderly Transition AI Alignment Multi-Planetary Backup

Core Question

During the upgrade from a carbon-based civilization to a silicon-carbon hybrid, how do we make sure we don't lose ourselves halfway through?

Philosophy

How We Do Research

Start from First Principles

We don't accept "because that's how it's done on Earth" as an answer. Every technical solution is re-derived from the physics up, ensuring it holds in the target environment.

Open Source Is the Starting Point, Not the Finish Line

We don't open-source to drop a link after the work is done. The entire research process — including failed attempts and abandoned directions — is shared in real time. So others can learn from our mistakes.

AI Does the Research, Humans Do the Judgment

AI handles processing massive information, finding cross-disciplinary connections, and generating candidate solutions. Humans ask the questions, set the direction, and make value judgments. Right now, humans are the steering wheel. AI is the engine.
